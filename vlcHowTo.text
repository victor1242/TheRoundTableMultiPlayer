What you described (camera -> capture box HDMI in, HDMI out to TV works, USB to laptop) usually means the HDMI pass-through is fine, but Windows/VLC is not picking up the USB capture stream correctly yet.

Try this in order:

1. Confirm Windows sees the capture device
- Plug in capture card USB directly to laptop (no hub if possible).
- Open Device Manager.
- Look under Cameras, Sound, video and game controllers, or Imaging devices.
- You should see something like USB Video, UVC Camera, HD Video Capture, etc.
- If you see unknown device, driver issue is likely.

2. Quick test outside VLC
- Open Windows Camera app.
- Switch camera source if needed.
- If you can see your camcorder video there, USB capture is working and VLC config is the issue.

3. Privacy settings (Windows 11)
- Settings -> Privacy & security -> Camera
- Turn on Camera access and Let desktop apps access your camera.
- VLC needs this permission.

4. VLC setup (important)
- Open VLC.
- Media -> Open Capture Device
- Capture mode: DirectShow
- Video device name: choose your USB capture device
- Audio device name: choose capture audio/HDMI audio device (if available)
- Video size: try 1920x1080 first, then 1280x720 if no image
- FPS: 30 (or 25 if your region uses PAL)
- Click Play

5. If video appears but audio is missing
- In VLC, try different Audio device entries (USB Audio, Digital Audio Interface, HDMI audio, etc.).
- Also check your camera playback audio is enabled and not muted.

6. If VLC still shows black/no signal
- Change camera output resolution (if possible) to 1080p or 720p (not 4K).
- Disable camera HDMI overlays/info display if enabled.
- Try another USB 3 port.
- Try another cable (especially mini-HDMI/HDMI from camera).
- Test in OBS Studio once: if OBS sees it but VLC doesn’t, it’s definitely VLC config.

7. Recording in VLC once preview works
- Media -> Open Capture Device -> (set device) -> click arrow next to Play -> Convert
- Profile: Video - H.264 + MP3 (MP4)
- Choose destination file and Start

Important note
- Some capture cards can only be opened by one app at a time. Close all other camera/video apps before opening in VLC.
- If TV passthrough works but PC never detects USB video in any app, the capture device may be non-UVC or defective.

Great progress. You’re correct: in VLC, clicking Play only previews the live capture, it does not automatically record.

To record in VLC:

1. Open Media -> Open Capture Device and select your capture device.
2. Click Play to confirm you see video.
3. Enable recording controls:
   1. View -> Advanced Controls
4. Click the red Record button to start capture.
5. Click the red Record button again to stop.
6. VLC saves the file to your Videos folder by default (unless you use Convert/Save and pick a path).

More reliable method (lets you choose filename first):

1. Media -> Open Capture Device
2. Click the small arrow next to Play -> Convert
3. Pick profile H.264 + MP3 (MP4)
4. Choose destination file path/name
5. Start

Quick tip for old tape/camcorder content:
- In VLC, enable deinterlacing during preview for smoother motion:
  Video -> Deinterlace -> On (or Automatic)
